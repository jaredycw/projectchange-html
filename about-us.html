Our Story

Project Change is an apolitical and non-profit initiative that seeks to support the more than 6,500 youths under 25 arrested during the 2019 Anti-Extradition Bill Movement and their loved ones in overcoming hardships.

introduction

Founded in 2020 by economist Prof. Yun-wing Sung (hyperlink - PC) and social work professor Dr. Pauline Sung (hyperlink - PC) , the idea of launching an initiative to help arrested youths came to mind at a Lunar New Year gathering, where the Sungs and their friends in academia shared how they each encountered students arrested during the protests, and felt that support for the youths was inadequate.

Appreciating that trust and credibility was key, the Sungs set out to launch Project Change via Citizen Action Design Limited, the think tank they founded, and went on to build a cross-disciplinary, professional team, with expertise in therapy, psychiatry, social work, mediation, and legal services.

Project Change advocates an education- and therapy-driven approach, instead of one that is excessively punitive. Our support involves holistic psychosocial counselling, legal support, and a comprehensive reintegration program, all aimed at aiding our youths in reintegrating into society. We have supported around 200 youths and their loved ones to date. Regardless of where you stand politically or ideologically, we hope you’d agree that the well-being of our future generations ought to be our priority.

About CADL

Citizen Action Design Limited (CADL)

is a non-profit policy research think tank established in Hong Kong. The think tank specialises in youth, family and early childhood investment policy research, and aims to provide a platform for aspiring young researchers to collaborate with well-established and experienced scholars in policy advocacy and research.

VISION, MISSION & COMMITMENT

Our Vision

Igniting hope for our youth is igniting hope for Hong Kong. We envision a Hong Kong where society and its youths reconcile and make peace.

Our Mission

Enable arrested youths to be able to see value in themselves, become contributing members of society, see hope in their future and continue to pursue excellence in life.
